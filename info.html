History

Scrabble is a word game that was invented in the United States by an architect named Alfred Mosher Butts in 1938. Originally called "Lexiko," the game was not successful until it was sold to James Brunot in 1948, who made some changes to the rules and design, and renamed it "Scrabble." It quickly became a hit and was sold to Selchow and Righter, a game company that expanded it into international versions and a deluxe edition. Hasbro now owns the rights to the game, which remains one of the most popular board games in the world, with millions of copies sold every year.

Rules

  1. Players draw tiles and take turns forming words on a board.
  2. Words score points based on the letters used.
  3. Special squares on the board increase scores.
  4. Using all seven tiles in one turn earns a bonus score.
  5. Players can challenge invalid words.
  6. The game ends when all tiles are used or all players pass twice..
  7. The player with the highest score wins.

Tips

  • Tip 1: Know the point value of each tile.
  • Tip 2: Use high-value letters on double/triple letter or word squares.
  • Tip 3: Look for opportunities to make multiple words at once.
  • Tip 4: Keep a balanced rack of vowels and consonants
  • Tip 5: Play defensively by blocking potential high-scoring squares.
